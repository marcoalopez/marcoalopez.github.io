About me: Hola, I’m Marco, an earth scientist in the Department of Geology at UniOvi (Spain). I hold a PhD in Geology from the University of Oviedo since 2013. After spending a few years away from academia, I joined Sergio’s Llana-Fúnez research group as a postdoc where my research focused mainly on characterizing rock microstructure through image analysis. From 2018 to the end of 2021, I worked as a postdoc in the Manteau et Interfaces research group at Géosciences Montpellier in France under the direction of Andréa Tommasi. First with an MSCA-COFUND grant to develop the project IDEA (2 years) and then as a CNRS researcher and member of the RhEoVOLUTION project. During this stage, my research focuses on characterizing the evolution of the strain field, microstructure and crystal preferred orientation during deformation and heating using in-situ monitoring, Electron Backscatter Diffraction (EBSD), and Digital Image Correlation (DIC) techniques. In 2022, I started a new research stage in Spain to develop the project OUTCROP as principal investigator.

I use observational, experimental and computational approaches to study the physical properties of Earth's solid materials (rock/mineral physics) and understanding the dynamic processes occurring in the Earth's lithosphere (the Earth's outer layer) from the microscopic to the orogenic scale. My primary interest is to understand how microstructure and crystal preferred orientation affect the mechanical and elastic properties of rocks, how rocks respond to an applied force (deformation and rheology) or the seismic/elastic properties of solid Earth materials. Other interests are crustal strength modelling, understanding strain localization at all scales, modelling of seismic properties in rocks, and the interaction between rock deformation, fluids, and metamorphism. I enjoy coding and am an advocate of open source and open science so I like to create small projects to promote earth sciences through programming. I also try to spread the word about my research interest through short blog posts, my project websites, and a YouTube channel called Earth science talks.
