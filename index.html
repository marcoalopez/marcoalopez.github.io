Short Bio and Research Interests

I am a former Ph.D. student at the University of Oviedo, Spain. I completed my Ph.D. in Geology at the University of Oviedo in July 2013. I am currently combining my work as a freelance geologist with scientific research in collaboration with my colleagues at the University of Oviedo, Autonomous University of Barcelona and others. I am currently looking for a postdoctoral position.

My Ph.D. thesis was focused on the study of a deep crustal-scale extensional shear zone involving geological mapping, structural geology, metamorphism (porphyroblast-matrix relations and P-T-t paths), the study of rock microstructure and deformation mechanisms (image analysis, SEM-BSD, SEM-EBSD, electron microprobe) and the interpretation of zircon U-Pb geochronological data.

My research interest range from different aspects of structural geology, metamorphic petrology and geochronology. My primary interests are rock deformation and rheology, rock microstructure (particularly grain size distributions) and the interaction between rock deformation (particularly shear zones) and metamorphism, but also the stablishment of the absolute time at which these processes occur. I also enjoy data mining , scientific computing and programming.
